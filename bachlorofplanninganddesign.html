Need for planning professionals

Planning courses are being run in almost 100 US universities, such as Cornell, Harvard, and University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, and UK universities such as Cardiff, London School of Economics. According to Janagrah (2010) around 3,000 planners are needed per year. In comparision, there are only 400 planners produced per year in India. Thus, there is a huge gap between demand and supply for professional planners.


Skills Imparted

This Bachelor of Planning course provides students with a critical understanding of issues that concern urban and regional planning. Planning is a balancing act between constructing modern communities and conserving our natural and built heritage to create diverse, vibrant and sustainable places where people want to live, work and play. The study of planning includes the technical knowledge that is required to identify urban problems, inculcating the values and attitudes of planning and commitment to the public who planners serve, and the skills to formulate, evaluate and implement urban planning policies at the national, regional, urban, rural and local scales. The broad themes that the undergraduate course will follow include timely themes such as creation of City Development Plans or Masters Plans, Urban Infrastructure Development, Traffic and Transportation Plans, Urban Finance and Accounting, Municipal Capacity Building, Environment and Development Planning (Climate Change, Environmental Impact Assessment, Disaster Management), Housing and Community Planning and Project Appraisal and Management.

Teaching Philosophy and Pedagogy Style

The mode of education in this undergraduate course is both lecture-oriented on theoretical and practical aspects of planning as well as engaging in planning studios exercises to maintain a dialectical between theory and praxis. Students are expected to be active participants in interactive sessions in class. Writing is the main tool employed for thinking, as a planner is both a practitioner and a scholar. Reading and writing beforehand and regularly, allows you to be better abreast with new developments and research trends, think critically and write coherently. The program encourages students to work collaboratively so as to build a community of scholars and planning practitioners. Collaborative efforts allow to learn from your peer’s strengths and complement their weakness.
